## New Insights from April 23 PM Scan

### 1. Gartner/Exiger — Supplier Risk Management Must Unify Across Functions (2026)
**Source:** Exiger citing Gartner Supply Chain Symposium/Xpo 2026, published April 22–23, 2026
**Promoted to MEMORY.md:** YES

Gartner projects 60% of supply chain disruptions resolved without human intervention by 2031. More immediately: supplier risk management remains siloed (Procurement = continuity, Compliance = sanctions, Logistics = disruption, Finance = cost) while the threat environment demands operational-speed coordination. Three convergence forces: (1) geopolitics as active operational variable; (2) AI narrowing the window for manual coordination; (3) concentration risk from lean sourcing.
- **Quantix lens:** Carrier network fragility (hazmat-credentialed drivers, terminal-specific relationships) mirrors concentrated supplier portfolio risk. Silo problem maps to safety/dispatch/compliance separation in bulk chemical fleets. Single unified view of carrier risk is the gap.

### 2. LSS DMAIC Applied to Logistics Service Processes — Throughput Time Reduction
**Source:** MDPI Logistics Vol. 9 No. 4 (Nov 2025) + ScienceDirect 3PL companion study (2023)
**Promoted to MEMORY.md:** YES (adds PCE benchmarking angle not yet in KB)

Peer-reviewed DMAIC application to administrative/service logistics processes (not just manufacturing) achieves same structured waste identification. Key: PCE benchmarking (value-added time ÷ total lead time) is an underused diagnostic. 3PL case: PCE improved from sub-20% to >40% post-DMAIC. Tools validated: SIPOC in Define, MSA in Measure, fishbone/5-Why in Analyze, control charts in Control.
- **Quantix lens:** Terminal driver check-in/check-out, load documentation, trailer inspection are all service logistics processes with strong DMAIC candidacy. PCE benchmarking is the entry diagnostic.

### 3. Chemical Logistics Sustainability — Intermodal, Visibility, Cargo Design (2026 Imperatives)
